# Weekly GTM Operating System (Client Command Center)

## 1) Why This Feature

### Problem
Most startup teams do not fail from lack of strategy. They fail from weak weekly execution:
- Priorities change too often.
- Teams run activity without clear ownership.
- Reporting is reactive instead of operational.
- Founders cannot see bottlenecks early enough to fix them.

### Opportunity
Build a weekly GTM operating layer that converts strategy into predictable execution:
- Monday: align priorities.
- Daily: execute and track blockers.
- Friday: review outcomes and reset next week.

This creates ongoing client value and improves retention by making results visible, measurable, and repeatable.

## 2) Goals

### Business Goals
- Increase client retention through operational stickiness.
- Improve campaign and pipeline outcomes through consistent weekly execution.
- Create a structured advisory workflow that scales across clients.
- Generate higher-quality case studies from measurable process improvements.

### User Goals
- Know the top 3 GTM priorities every week.
- Assign clear owners and deadlines for critical actions.
- Track leading indicators before revenue issues appear.
- Finish each week with a clear next-step plan.

## 3) Scope

### In Scope (V1)
- Weekly planning board (Top 3 priorities).
- Action cards with owner, due date, and expected impact.
- KPI panel with weekly deltas.
- Blocker log with severity and owner.
- Monday plan generator.
- Friday review summary.
- Downloadable weekly brief (Markdown/PDF-ready).
- Client-facing dashboard page.
- Event instrumentation.

### Out of Scope (V1)
- Full CRM synchronization.
- Multi-workspace enterprise admin controls.
- Automated Slack/Email notifications.
- AI-generated recommendations based on external tools.

## 5) UX and Interaction Design

### UX Principles
- Keep planning to under 15 minutes on Monday.
- Keep daily updates to under 5 minutes.
- Keep Friday review to under 10 minutes.
- Make blockers and owner accountability explicit.

### Core Flows
1. User opens weekly workspace.
2. User sets top priorities and owners.
3. Team executes and updates progress during week.
4. User logs blockers and mitigation actions.
5. User completes Friday review and next-week carryover.

## 6) Data Model

### Key Entities
- `OpsWorkspace`
- `OpsWeek`
- `PriorityItem`
- `ActionCard`
- `KPIRecord`
- `Blocker`
- `WeeklyBrief`

### KPI Categories (V1)
- Demand: sessions, qualified leads, CTR.
- Pipeline: SQLs, meetings booked, stage conversion.
- Revenue signal: opportunity value, close velocity.
- Delivery signal: on-time execution rate.

### Weekly Status Logic
- `On Track`: >= 70% of actions on schedule and no high-severity blocker unresolved.
- `At Risk`: < 70% on schedule or one unresolved high-severity blocker.
- `Off Track`: repeated slip + KPI decline two consecutive weeks.

## 7) Recommendation and Review Logic

### Monday Planning Logic
- Require top 3 priorities.
- Each priority must have one owner and one measurable outcome.
- Each priority maps to a KPI category.

### Friday Review Logic
- Mark each priority as `Done`, `Partial`, or `Missed`.
- Capture reason code for partial/missed.
- Auto-generate carryover list for next week.

### Insight Layer
- Flag recurring blockers.
- Highlight priorities that lacked KPI movement.
- Surface "execution risk score" for next week.

## 9) Analytics and Success Measurement

### Event Taxonomy
- `ops_week_started`
- `ops_priority_created`
- `ops_action_completed`
- `ops_blocker_logged`
- `ops_friday_review_completed`
- `ops_weekly_brief_downloaded`

### Event Properties
- `client_id`
- `week_id`
- `priority_count`
- `on_time_rate`
- `blocker_count`
- `week_status`

### Primary KPI
- Weekly completion rate: `ops_friday_review_completed / ops_week_started`

### Secondary KPIs
- Priority completion rate.
- Action on-time rate.
- Blocker resolution time.
- Week-over-week KPI delta consistency.

## 11) Rollout Plan

### Phase A: Internal Pilot (2-3 clients)
- Validate weekly workflow and UX friction.
- Calibrate status logic and reason-code taxonomy.

### Phase B: Select Client Rollout (5-10 clients)
- Standardize weekly rhythm templates.
- Capture baseline completion and KPI movement.

### Phase C: Full Program
- Deploy across active retainers.
- Add benchmarking across client cohorts.
- Package into premium operating advisory.

## 12) Risks and Mitigations

- **Risk:** Teams do not update consistently.
  - **Mitigation:** lightweight daily interactions and weekly rhythm templates.
- **Risk:** Tool becomes task-heavy.
  - **Mitigation:** cap weekly priorities at 3 and minimize required fields.
- **Risk:** KPI interpretation varies by client.
  - **Mitigation:** stage-specific KPI presets and clear metric definitions.
- **Risk:** Clients focus on activity over outcomes.
  - **Mitigation:** require measurable outcomes per priority and Friday result classification.

## 13) Success Criteria (First 8 Weeks)

- >= 75% weekly review completion.
- >= 70% on-time action completion.
- >= 20% reduction in recurring blocker count.
- Positive KPI movement in at least 2 of 4 KPI categories per client.
