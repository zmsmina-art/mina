# AI Startup Growth Index (Founder Circle Beta)

## 1) Why This Feature

### Problem
Early-stage founders are forced to make GTM decisions with low-quality benchmarks:
- Public benchmark reports are too broad and usually not AI-specific.
- Most founders cannot tell if they have a "channel problem," "narrative problem," or "conversion problem."
- Teams optimize vanity metrics because there is no stage-specific comparison baseline.

### Opportunity
Create a founder-only benchmark product that turns private cohort data into:
- Clear stage-adjusted percentiles.
- Actionable weekly priorities.
- A repeatable GTM operating rhythm.

This gives selected founders direct strategic value while creating a durable authority asset for Mina's long-term knowledge and trust goals.

## 2) Goals

### Business Goals
- Position Mina as a data-backed GTM operator for AI startups.
- Increase qualified strategy calls from founders that match ideal client profile.
- Generate original benchmark IP that compounds quarterly.
- Build a private founder cohort product that can later expand into paid advisory.

### User Goals
- Understand where they stand versus relevant AI startup peers.
- Identify highest-leverage fixes for the next 30-90 days.
- Leave with a practical operator brief they can execute with their team.

## 3) Audience and Access Model

### Target Users (Beta)
- Founder/CEO or GTM owner at AI startups (pre-seed to Series A).
- Teams with early traction and active GTM motion.
- Invited via direct link or access code.

### Access Rules (V1)
- Invite-only onboarding.
- No anonymous public benchmarking dashboard.
- Public-facing report pages show only aggregated cohort insights.

## 4) Scope

### In Scope (V1)
- Founder intake form (company stage, segment, GTM model, team size, geography).
- Metric submission flow with strict definitions.
- Stage- and model-adjusted benchmarking engine.
- Percentile output by GTM dimension.
- "What to fix next" recommendation layer.
- Downloadable founder brief (Markdown/PDF).
- Quarterly public report page with anonymized insights.
- Event instrumentation and completion analytics.

### Out of Scope (V1)
- Full CRM integration.
- Multi-user workspace collaboration.
- API access for external analytics tools.
- Real-time continuous benchmark refresh.
- Paid plan and billing logic.

## 5) Core Value Proposition

Founders get:
- A private GTM scorecard built from peer data.
- Immediate prioritization (not generic advice).
- A 30/60/90-day execution lens.

Mina gets:
- Repeated high-intent founder touchpoints.
- Proprietary benchmark dataset.
- Strong authority signals through recurring public reports.

## 7) UX and Interaction Design

### UX Principles
- Keep completion under 12 minutes.
- Eliminate ambiguity with metric definition tooltips.
- Show confidence and data quality level with each result.
- Translate percentiles into concrete weekly operating actions.

### Core Flows
1. Founder accesses invite link.
2. Founder confirms profile and stage.
3. Founder submits standardized metrics.
4. System validates quality and computes benchmarks.
5. Founder receives scorecard + priority actions + downloadable brief.
6. Founder can book a strategy session from results screen.

## 8) Data Model

### Key Entities
- `FounderProfile`
- `CompanyProfile`
- `MetricDefinition`
- `MetricSubmission`
- `Cohort`
- `BenchmarkResult`
- `ActionRecommendation`
- `QuarterlySnapshot`

### GTM Dimensions
- Positioning Clarity
- ICP Precision
- Demand Capture
- Funnel Conversion
- Pipeline Efficiency
- Revenue Quality
- Operating Cadence

### Scoring and Percentiles
- Raw metrics are normalized by stage and GTM model.
- Each dimension receives a 0-100 score.
- Overall score is weighted by stage.
- Percentile bands:
  - `0-24`: At Risk
  - `25-49`: Below Cohort
  - `50-74`: Competitive
  - `75-100`: Outperforming

## 9) Data Trust, Privacy, and Guardrails

### Data Quality
- Reject incomplete submissions.
- Validate units/ranges before scoring.
- Flag outliers for manual review.

### Privacy and Publication Rules
- Never expose company-identifiable raw metrics publicly.
- Public report requires minimum cohort size threshold (ex: n >= 7 per segment).
- Aggregate and anonymize all public insight blocks.
- Allow founders to opt out of future aggregated reporting.

## 10) Recommendation Logic

### Output Structure
- Top 2 bottlenecks (lowest percentile dimensions).
- Top 2 strengths (highest percentile dimensions).
- 3 immediate actions for next 14 days.
- 30/60/90-day execution sequence.
- KPI watchlist with leading indicators.

### Action Mapping
- Low Positioning + low conversion -> narrative + proof system sprint.
- Good demand + weak close rate -> sales process and objection architecture sprint.
- Weak cadence -> weekly GTM operating system and KPI ownership sprint.

## 14) Rollout Plan (Founder-Only)

### Phase A: Private Alpha (10-15 founders)
- Manual invite list.
- Validate metric definitions and completion UX.
- Calibrate scoring and recommendation quality.

### Phase B: Closed Beta (25-40 founders)
- Launch quarterly report v1 from anonymized dataset.
- Add quality confidence indicators and clearer action cards.
- Track conversion and call quality.

### Phase C: Controlled Expansion
- Expand invite volume by segment.
- Add follow-up submission cadence (quarterly re-benchmark).
- Evaluate monetization paths (advisory tier or premium benchmark access).

## 15) Risks and Mitigations

- **Risk:** Founders submit inconsistent data.
  - **Mitigation:** strict metric definitions, validation checks, outlier handling.
- **Risk:** Not enough cohort density for useful percentiles.
  - **Mitigation:** stage/segment gating and minimum sample thresholds before publishing.
- **Risk:** Results feel abstract.
  - **Mitigation:** convert each weak dimension into owner-assigned weekly actions.
- **Risk:** Privacy concerns reduce participation.
  - **Mitigation:** explicit anonymization policy and opt-out controls.

## 16) Success Criteria (First 90 Days)

- 20+ completed founder submissions.
- >= 60% completion rate from verified invites.
- >= 20% results-to-booking click-through.
- 1 public quarterly benchmark report published.
- At least 3 external mentions/citations of the report.
